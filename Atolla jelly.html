    • Atolla Jellyfish

    Atolla jellyfish or Coronate medusa. They are found in any deep water. It is deep red in colour and has many small tentacles but only 20 long tentacles. These are used mostly to capture pray and swim at high speeds. This jellyfish is also bioluminescent and sends flashes when attacked this helps in confusing the predator. That is why it is also called alarm jellyfish. The bell of the jelly is around 20 to 174 millimetres in diameter.

    They mostly prey on floating nutrients and crustaceans. The atolla jellyfish is slightly endangered due to the increasing numbers of shrimp; their predators and capturing of the fish from the deep-sea by locals to be used as delicacy. A living thing emitting visible light is called bioluminescence. Marine life found in the deep water frequently exhibits bioluminescence. In order to protect itself from predators, Atolla Wyvillei has modified a safety reaction. Attacking Atolla wyvillei causes it to flash a variety of blue lights.

    These flashes move at a velocity of 5 to 50 cm/s and move in circular waves (Herring 2004). Atolla wyvillei is known as "alarm jelly" because of these blue flashes. The lights are thought to be used to draw larger predators than the one that was already pursuing it. It's an effort to frighten the predator that is currently assaulting it with a larger predator that could potentially prey on the predator attacking it. Atolla wyvillei can reproduce in two different ways. They can reproduce asexually like many other jellyfish species. This process involves the development into polyps that then produce buds that grow into larvae.

    Atolla wyvillei can also reproduce sexually. They attach themselves to another Atolla wyvillei by grabbing them with their hypertrophied tentacle and pulling themselves toward the other to mate. Crustaceans and other nutrient-floating organisms have been discovered to be the primary prey of Atolla wyvillei. With the help of its elongated tentacle, Atolla wyvillei may ensnare its victim. By leaving the tentacle extended, it can capture prey that may be floating nearby while also passively catching it.
